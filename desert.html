Algerian Desert

The Algerian Desert, also known as the Sahara Desert in Algeria, is a vast, arid region located in North Africa. It is the largest hot desert in the world, covering a substantial portion of Algeria's territory. The Algerian Desert is characterized by its extreme aridity, with very low annual rainfall, high temperatures, and expansive stretches of sand dunes, rocky plateaus, and barren landscapes.

The natural environment

The Algerian Desert hosts a diverse range of wildlife, including reptiles and mammals adapted to the harsh environment. Various plant species also thrive in some arid regions that are well-suited to dry conditions.

Sefar in Algeria

Algeria is home to one of the most important ancient abandoned cities in the midst of the desert, which is Timgad. Timgad is an ancient Roman city that was established in the 1st century AD. It was built by the order of the Roman Emperor Trajan and served as a Roman settlement in the heart of the desert
